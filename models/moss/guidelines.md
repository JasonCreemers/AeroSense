# Plant Care Guidelines

## Sensor Ranges
- **Temperature:** 65-80°F ideal. Below 55°F slows growth. Above 85°F causes heat stress.
- **Humidity:** 40-60% RH ideal. Below 30% causes crispy tips. Above 70% risks mold.
- **Water Level:** 30-80mm healthy. 80-100mm refill soon. Over 100mm critical.
- **Light:** 10-16 hours/day for herbs and leafy greens. Plants need a dark period.

## Health Classes
- **Healthy:** Normal. No action needed.
- **Underwatered:** Drooping leaves, dry roots. Check reservoir, increase misting.
- **Overwatered:** Yellow lower leaves, soft stems, slimy roots. Reduce misting frequency.
- **More_Light:** Leggy, stretched, pale growth. Extend light schedule.
- **Less_Light:** Leaf edges burn, upper leaves yellow. Reduce light hours.
- **Nutrient_Burn:** Brown crispy leaf tips with yellow halo. Flush system, reduce nutrients.
- **Pathogen:** Disease detected — mildew, rot, or lesions. Isolate affected plants, improve airflow.

## Vision Classes
- **Chlorosis:** Yellowing. Causes: nutrient deficiency, overwatering, pH imbalance.
- **Necrosis:** Dead brown/black tissue. Causes: nutrient burn, infection, temperature stress.
- **Pest:** Holes, stippling, webbing. Common: aphids, spider mites, fungus gnats.
- **Tip Burn:** Brown crispy leaf tips. Causes: high nutrients, low humidity, calcium deficiency.
- **Wilting:** Drooping leaves. Causes: underwatering, heat stress, root rot.

## Key Aeroponic Tips
- Roots need oxygen. If too wet, they suffocate — the top cause of root rot.
- Healthy roots are white and fluffy. Brown or slimy roots indicate a problem.
- After any change, give plants 3-7 days to respond before adjusting again.
